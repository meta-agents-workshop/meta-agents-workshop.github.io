Proposal

Agents Optimizing Agents

Toward the Responsible Use of Meta-Agents that Build, Manage, and Optimize Other Agents

NeurIPS 2026 · December 11-12, 2026 · Sydney, Australia

Agents that design, optimize, train, and oversee other agents.

About The Workshop

For the past two years, progress on language agents has come from making a single agent do more: browse the web, write and run code, call tools, and stay on task for hours. The next frontier is the meta level, the agent that acts on other agents.

We call these higher-order agents meta-agents: agents that design, optimize, supervise, and train other agents. This is already happening, but it is split across communities that rarely talk:

  • Automated design of agent architectures and the harness that wraps them (ADAS, Meta Agent Search, automated harness design).
  • Agents that recursively improve their own code (the Darwin Gödel Machine, self-taught optimizers).
  • Optimizers that compile and tune multi-module language-model programs (DSPy, GEPA).
  • The meta-learning tradition that asks how systems learn to learn.

This workshop brings these threads under one roof and asks the question none of them has owned: how do we build and improve agents systematically, increasingly using agents to do it, and how do we keep a recursively self-improving system under control? It builds on the team's 1st Multi-Turn Interaction Workshop @ NeurIPS 2025.



Topics

We organize submissions around five themes. Topics include but are not limited to:

(1) Automated Design and Discovery of Agentic Systems:

Most agent systems are still built by hand. We invite work on searching the space of agent architectures, tools, and scaffolds (Meta Agent Search, automated harness design); on telling when a discovered design generalizes past the tasks it was tuned on; and on the cost of running these searches at scale.

(2) Optimizing Compound AI Systems and Language-Model Programs:

Once an agent is more than one model call, the problem is optimizing the whole program. We seek work on optimizers for multi-module systems (DSPy, GEPA, Trace), credit assignment across modules, inference-time search, context optimization, and the theory of when any of this converges.

(3) Self-Improvement and Open-Ended Evolution:

A meta-agent can point at itself. We welcome work on agents that recursively rewrite their own code (the Darwin Gödel Machine, self-taught optimizers), AI-generating algorithms and open-endedness, self-play and co-evolution, and honest analyses of how self-improving agents drift or game their own reward.

(4) Meta-Learning and Meta-RL for Agents:

Learning how to learn predates language agents and gains new surface area here. We invite work connecting classical meta-learning and meta-RL to agent training: learned optimizers, learned reward and verification, tree-structured rollouts and finer-grained credit assignment, and curricula one agent proposes for another.

(5) Substrates, Evaluation, and Oversight for Meta-Agents:

Acting on another agent means watching, forking, and replaying its execution. We invite work on runtimes that treat agent execution as a first-class object, benchmarks for meta-level methods (not just agents doing tasks), and the oversight problem that self-improving and agent-designed systems create.



Call For Papers

Agents Optimizing Agents (NeurIPS 2026) invites submissions on architectures, algorithms, theory, empirical studies, benchmarks, and position papers about agents that design, optimize, supervise, train, or improve other agents. Submissions must present original, unpublished work that has not appeared at NeurIPS or other archival machine-learning venues.

Key Dates

  • Submission Deadline: August 29, 2026, AoE (tentative)
  • Notification: on or before September 29, 2026, AoE
  • Workshop Date: December 11-12, 2026 (Sydney)
All deadlines follow the Anywhere on Earth (AoE) timezone. Final dates will be confirmed upon workshop acceptance and aligned with the NeurIPS 2026 timeline.

Submission Site

Submissions will be managed via OpenReview and remain private during review. All authors should maintain up-to-date OpenReview profiles for conflict-of-interest management and paper matching. The submission portal will be announced once the workshop is accepted.

Scope

We welcome contributions across the five themes above. Accepted papers are presented as posters, with a subset selected for oral or spotlight talks. The workshop is in person at NeurIPS 2026 in Sydney.

Submission Guidelines

Formatting
Submissions must be in English and use the NeurIPS 2026 workshop LaTeX template. Papers are submitted as a single PDF:
  • Full Papers: at most 8 pages (main text)
  • Short Papers: at most 4 pages (main text)
  • Tiny Papers: encouraged from students and under-resourced groups, welcoming simple ideas, modest results, follow-ups, or fresh perspectives.
  • Demo Track: live demonstrations of meta-agent systems and tools, presented alongside the poster sessions.
  • References and appendices do not count toward the page limit, but the main text must be self-contained.
Meta-Agent Card

Every submission includes a one-page Meta-Agent Card: what the method optimizes and against which signal, the scope of change it makes to the agent it acts on, how a deployer would test that the result generalizes, the failure modes specific to acting on other agents (reward gaming, drift, brittle self-edits, runaway search), and the guardrails or rollback that catch them. The card is reviewed alongside the technical contribution, and we run a pre-deadline clinic to help newer and under-resourced groups complete it.

Anonymity
The workshop uses double-blind review. Submissions must be anonymized, with author names, affiliations, and acknowledgments removed and prior work cited in the third person.

Non-Archival Policy

The workshop is non-archival. Papers under review elsewhere are welcome, and accepted papers may be published at other venues afterward. Work already published at NeurIPS or other archival ML venues should not be submitted.
